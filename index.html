Research Focus

My work begins with a fundamental question: In a world of digital abundance, why is genuine connection in physical spaces so scarce?

I'm building the protocol that answers this. Think of it as a social discovery layer for the physical world.

While traditional maps show you where things are, our protocol reveals where meaningful conversations and intellectual collisions are happening.

The result? An art gallery transforms from a silent repository of objects into a vibrant salon of ideas. A academic conference becomes a live network graph, connecting people across disciplines around shared curiosities. A corporate office fosters true cross-pollination, moving beyond org charts.
